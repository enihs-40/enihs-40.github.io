Project Overview

The knee scooter capstone project involved complete product development from initial concept through detailed CAD modeling, assembly design, and manufacturing-ready drawings. This mobility aid device was designed to provide enhanced comfort and maneuverability for individuals recovering from lower leg injuries.

Our team focused on creating an ergonomic, stable, and user-friendly design that could be manufactured cost-effectively while meeting all safety requirements for medical mobility devices.

Design Process

Concept Development: Began with user research and ergonomic studies to understand the needs of individuals using mobility aids. Created multiple concept sketches exploring different frame geometries and adjustment mechanisms.

CAD Modeling: Developed detailed 3D models in SolidWorks for all major components including the frame, handlebar assembly, knee platform, and wheel mechanisms. Applied proper GD&T standards to all critical dimensions.

Assembly Design: Created full assembly with proper mates and constraints. Ensured all moving parts had appropriate clearances and that the design could be easily manufactured and assembled.

Technical Documentation: Generated complete manufacturing drawings including bill of materials, detailed part drawings with dimensions and tolerances, and assembly instructions.

Technical Details

  • Materials: Aluminum 6061-T6 for frame components, stainless steel hardware, polyurethane wheels
  • Dimensions: Adjustable height range 32"-42", knee platform width 8", overall length 38"
  • Features: Tool-free height adjustment, dual braking system, foldable design for storage
  • Standards: Complies with FDA medical device regulations and ASTM standards for mobility aids
  • Manufacturing: Designed for aluminum extrusion and welding with minimal machining operations

Key Learnings

This capstone project provided valuable experience in complete product development workflow. Working with a team taught me effective collaboration strategies and the importance of clear communication when sharing CAD files and design decisions.

The most challenging aspect was balancing manufacturing constraints with design optimization. We iterated through several frame designs before arriving at a solution that met both structural requirements and cost targets.

Moving forward, I gained confidence in my ability to take a project from concept through detailed design documentation, which will be valuable in my engineering career.
